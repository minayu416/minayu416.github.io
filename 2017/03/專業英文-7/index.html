專業英文

March 23, 2017 in Course Note

Autobiographies analyze and interpret the events in a person’s life.

They try to find connections, explain the meaning of unexpected actions or mysteries, and make arguments about the significance of the person’s accomplishment or life activities.

Tips for Writing Autobiographies

  • Don’t exactly copy anyone else’s autobiography and avoid copying a template out of a book.

  • Make yourself stand out by having a catchy beginning.

  • Avoid using your name and birth date to begin your autobiography. A quotation, a few questions, or a meaningful personal story is more likely to catch an employer’s eye.

  • Try to sell yourself in the first paragraph.

  • You need to have a thesis in the first paragraph that shows you are highly qualified for this job because of your unique qualifications.

  • Fill in the missing information that the resume fails to cover.

  • Don’t try to cover your personality traits, family background, academic background, skills, work experience, and hobbies in detail. You only need to include the parts that are related to your thesis

  • Customize every autobiography. 

  • Do some research on every company and provide all requested information, so that you can explain why you are a good fit for this particular job. don’t send the same autobiography to different companies.

  • Don’t be too vague

  • Try to use specific example to support what you say

  • Keep your autobiography short and to the point.

  • Your autobiography should be limited to one pages, no more.

  • Make your autobiography look professional and interesting.

  • Carefully proofread your autobiography. Make sure there are no spelling, punctuation, or grammar errors.

  • Conclude your autobiography with a brief summary and a memorable statement.

註：stand out 突出

Organizational Style

  • Many biographies are written in chronological order.

  • Some group time periods around a major theme or ambition and achievement

  • Still other focus on specific topics or accomplishments.

註：chronological 時間序列的，按年代排列的

  • 人生遇到的困難:像是怎樣的事件打到腦袋讓自己本來討厭程式意味到自己熱愛程式，進而想申請Computer Science 研究所之類的。

How to write an autobiography in 4 paragraphs

When writing an autobiography, you focus on three major things:

  • who you are in life (personal information, 申請工作：education, 強調語言能力)

  • what life means to you (申請研究所：education, work experience, 可寫可不寫：other)

  • what your outlook on the future is (future life, 希望在這份工作或者這個學校的將來學習哪些，將來要做些什麼(類似讀書計畫))

  • Conclusion or brief summary

1st Who you are in life

The best way to start an autobiography is to state your name.

When you are writing this paragraph, you usually explain the type of person you are; use facts about yourself such as:

  • Have you won any awards? What types of awards have you won?

  • Did you finish school?

  • Do you plan on going to college?

Example: I was born (被生出來)，缺點：可以舉家庭互動但不要寫太長，forget to write name.

下次的單字

  • Adapt (v.)變化,適應

Some species in South America can’t adapt climate change by el niño almost been extinct.

  • Breed (v.) 繁殖

These two animals are different species can not mix breed together.

  • Climate (n.) 氣候

The climate condition is one factor caused desertification in this area.

  • Diversity (n.) 多樣化

Scientist show that the bio-diversity in low latitude is high than in high latitude.

  • Elevation (n.) 高度 海拔

Lima is one of the high elevation city in Peru.

  • Flourish (v.) 茂盛

This type of plant flourishes in tropical countries.

  • Graze (v.) 放牧

Overgrazing in an area will caused reduction of the vegetation cover.

  • Hatch (v/n)孵化孵出

The eggs are hatched by the heat of the sun.

  • Immigrate (v)移民

Since this area is dangerous so we immigrate to another area.

  • Phenomenon (n.)事件(單數名詞)

“Female insects release pheromone to attract male insects ”, is a biological phenomenon.

最後補充

寫文章可使用

  1. First, Second, Third, Last

  2. Firstly, Secondly, Thirdly, Finally

  3. the first, the second, the third, the last

  4. in the first place, in the second place, in the third place, finally

  5. to begin with, then, furthermore, final

  6. to start with, next, in addition, finally

  7. first, and foremost, besides, last but not least
